I think this is the right place to spend time. The winner won't necessarily have the most features—it'll have the **best emotional loop**.

Let's forget technology for a moment.

## Day 1

A user hears about the app.

They open it.

What's the first thing they should think?

Not:

> "Oh, another World Cup app."

Instead:

> **"I can prove I know football better than everyone else."**

Immediately, they understand the game.

---

# User Story

Let's call him James.

James watches football casually with his friends.

He always says:

> "Brazil is definitely winning this."

Your app asks him to prove it.

---

## Opening Screen

Instead of asking him to sign up immediately, show today's fixtures.

```
Today's Matches

🇧🇷 Brazil vs Spain 🇪🇸

Starts in 2h 15m

Predict →
```

One obvious action.

---

## Prediction Screen

Keep it playful.

```
Who wins?

○ Brazil
○ Draw
○ Spain
```

Next.

```
Score?

Brazil [2]

```

Next.

```
First Goal Scorer?

○ Vinicius
○ Rodrygo
○ Yamal
○ Morata
```

Done.

Don't overwhelm them with 20 prediction categories.

---

## The Hook

After submitting:

```
Prediction Locked 🔒

Potential Points

Winner      100
Score       300
Scorer      200

Maximum

600 pts
```

Now they're invested.

---

## Waiting

This is where many apps fail.

Don't just show:

> Match starts in 2 hours.

Instead:

```
Your prediction

Brazil 2–1 Spain

73% of players disagree with you.

🔥 Bold prediction.
```

Or

```
Only 4% predicted Spain.

You're taking a risk.
```

Now there's tension before kickoff.

---

## During the Match

This should feel alive.

```
⚽ GOAL

Spain scores.

❌ Your prediction is now in danger.

Chance of full points:
18%
```

Or

```
⚽ GOAL

Brazil scores.

You're on track!

Current prediction accuracy:
83%
```

The app becomes a companion while watching.

# What keeps people coming back?

This is the key question.

I'd identify four motivations:

### "I don't want to miss today's predictions."

Daily habit.

---

### "I wonder if I climbed."

Leaderboard.

---

### "I'm on a streak."

Loss aversion.

Nobody wants to lose a 12-day streak.

---

### "I can beat my friends."

Social competition.

---

# One thing I'd add

Instead of making every prediction worth the same...

Give users **one "Lock of the Day."**

They choose one prediction they're most confident about.

```
LOCK 🔒

Brazil beats Spain
```

If they're right:

2× points.

If they're wrong:

0 points.

Now every day has a dramatic decision.

---

# I think there's an even stronger UX

The app shouldn't feel like you're filling out a form.

It should feel like you're making bold calls on live television.

Imagine swiping through cards:

```
🇧🇷 Brazil vs Spain

Brazil wins

Swipe →
```

```
Score?

2–1

Swipe →
```

```
First scorer?

Vinicius

Swipe →
```

Three swipes. You're done in under 30 seconds.

That matters because most users won't spend five minutes making predictions before every match.

---

## One last thought

If we distill the experience to its essence, I think the app has a single job:

> **Transform passive viewers into emotionally invested participants.**

Whether they know a lot about football or just enjoy the World Cup, every notification, prediction, and point should make them feel, "This match matters because *I'm* part of it."

That's the kind of UX I'd optimize relentlessly. The live data isn't the product—it's the engine that makes the user's choices feel consequential.
